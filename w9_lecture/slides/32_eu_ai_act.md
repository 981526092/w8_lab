# EU AI Act

## What is it?
- A comprehensive legislative framework by the European Commission to regulate AI systems within the EU
- Goal: establish an ecosystem of trust — **safe, transparent, respects fundamental human rights**

## Who does it apply to?
- **Providers** (developers) of AI systems placed on the EU market
- **Deployers** (users) of AI systems within the EU
- Providers/deployers located **outside the EU** if output is used in the EU

## Risk-based classification:

| Risk Level | Regulatory Requirement | Examples |
|-----------|----------------------|----------|
| **Unacceptable risk** | Prohibited (Art. 5) | Social scoring, facial recognition, dark-pattern AI manipulation |
| **High risk** | Conformity Assessment (Art. 6 & ss.) | Education, employment, justice, immigration, law |
| **Limited risk** | Transparency (Art. 52) | Chatbots, deep fakes, emotion recognition systems |
| **Minimal risk** | Code of conduct (Art. 69) | Spam filters, video games |

## General-Purpose AI (GPAI):
- Models trained with large amounts of data with broad applicability (e.g., GPT, Claude)
- Must provide technical documentation, comply with copyright law, publish training data summaries
- GPAI with **systemic risk** (>10^25 FLOPs): additional obligations including adversarial testing

## Penalties:
- Up to **€35M or 7% of global annual turnover** for prohibited AI practices
- Up to **€15M or 3%** for other violations
