# NYC Local Law 144 — Case Study

## What is it?
- A law regulating the use of **Automated Employment Decision Tools (AEDT)** in New York City
- An AEDT is any computational process that uses ML, AI, or statistical modelling to substantially assist or replace discretionary decision-making in employment

## Key Requirements:
- **Bias audit** must be conducted by an independent auditor no more than one year prior to use
- Employers must **publish results** of the bias audit on their website
- Candidates must be **notified** that an AEDT is being used, with 10 business days notice

## Disparate Impact & the Four-Fifths Rule:
- **Impact Ratio** = Selection Rate (group a) / Selection Rate (group b)
- For **classification** (hire/no-hire): Selection Rate = proportion selected in each group
- For **scoring** (numerical rating): Scoring Rate = proportion scoring above median
- **Four-Fifths Rule**: If Impact Ratio < 0.8 (i.e., 4/5), there is evidence of **adverse impact**

## Why it matters:
- First US law to specifically regulate AI in hiring decisions
- Sets a precedent for AI accountability in employment
