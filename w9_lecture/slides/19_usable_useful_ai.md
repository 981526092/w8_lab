# Usable AI, Useful AI & Trustworthy AI

## Usable AI:
- An AI solution that is **easy to learn and use** via effective HCI design
- Example: ChatGPT's simple chat interface made LLMs accessible to millions overnight

## Useful AI:
- An AI solution that **satisfies target users' actual needs**
- Example: A medical AI that achieves 99% accuracy but takes 30 minutes per scan is useless to ER doctors who need results in seconds

## Trustworthy AI:
- An imperfect AI is likely to be **rejected** unless a reasonable level of trust is generated
- Properties: **Accuracy, Robustness, Fairness, Accountability, Transparency, Explainability, Ethical**

## How the three properties diverge in practice:

| System | Usable? | Useful? | Trustworthy? |
|--------|---------|---------|--------------|
| ChatGPT | Yes (simple chat) | Depends on task | Limited (hallucinations, no citations) |
| IBM Watson Health | No (complex interface) | Potentially (cancer diagnosis) | Partially (opaque reasoning) |
| Google Translate | Yes | Yes (travel, comms) | Partially (no confidence indicators) |

## The tension:
- Most deployed AI optimises for **usability** first — usefulness and trustworthiness are afterthoughts
- A model can be **usable** (easy UI) but not **useful** (doesn't solve the right problem)
- A model can be **useful** but not **trustworthy** (gives good results but can't explain why)
- Ideal: all three — usable, useful, AND trustworthy
