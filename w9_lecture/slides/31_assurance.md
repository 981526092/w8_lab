# Key Components of Algorithm Auditing — Assurance

## Outcome of audit / assurance processes:
After applying mitigation strategies, assess conformance to regulatory, governance and ethical standards → exhibit trustworthiness

## Components:
- **General and sector-specific assurance:** national (US NIST, UK ICO, EU AI Act), sectoral (finance: SEC, FCA; health: NIH, NHS)
- **Governance:** technical assessments (robustness, privacy) and impact assessments (risk, compliance)
- **Unknown risks:** risk schemes and **red-teaming**
- **Monitoring interfaces:** risk assessments and "traffic-light" interfaces
- **Certification:** of the system or the AI engineers themselves (i.e., training)

## Real-world case: South Wales Police facial recognition (2020)
- The **UK ICO** (Information Commissioner's Office) investigated **South Wales Police's** live facial recognition trials
- The ICO's opinion: the use **did not fully meet the threshold** for lawful use under data protection law
- Key issues: insufficient evidence of necessity & proportionality, inadequate Data Protection Impact Assessment
- Outcome: ICO issued formal opinion requiring **stricter safeguards** before further deployment
- Demonstrates why assurance is not optional — regulators can and do intervene
